Sobriety is Freedom

One in fourteen Americans reports experiences issues with substance abuse. Addiction often develops with individuals to cope with stress, loss, trauma or mental illness but it can have a devastating effect on their lives. When an individual partakes in an addictive substance, the brain's reward system is over flooded. It would now cause urges in a person to continue to repeat behaviors that feel good but are not healthy for them. The brain then adapts to the continued use to develop a tolerance, or using more of the substance to achieve the same result (cdc.gov).

Many associate addiction solely with a dependency on drugs and alcohol, yet addictive behaviors follow the same process as substance based addictions. Accordingly, they can also result in many problems in an individuals life. The elements of addiction or addictive behavior include:

  • One cannot stop the behavior
  • One cannot stop thinking about it
  • One has trouble managing their emotion
  • It interferes with one's daily life, relationships, or work life
  • One has less and less awareness of the negative consequences

Addiction is a disease, but not a character flaw. Overcoming addiction and addictive behaviors is not as simple as avoiding temptation. Sobriety requires an individual to continually break the feedback loop and the negative reward system of the chemical imbalance in their brain. Maintaining sobriety for an individual can truly be life changing. Nevertheless, between 40-60 % of recovering addicts will inevitably relapse. Therefore there is a need to learn and implement the proper tools for individuals to maintain their sobriety. Looking for a way to help individuals maintain their sobriety in the age of social media and digital resources helped provide the basis for the problem statement:

How might we make sobriety resources more convenient and accessible to anyone who could benefit from it?

Some individuals are not sure where to start when it comes to getting help, and some need help being consistent with their goals. The solution is to design an app that help users stay accountable to their goals, connected to a community, and provides them with a safe space to deal with the source of that behavior. An outlet like this can help users know they are not alone and they have more options than continuing any destructive behaviors. The Sobered Health app was designed in the frame of mind that relapse does not have to be a part of recovery.

Sobered, is targeted towards any individuals who are struggling to maintain sobriety with substance abuse or addictive behavior. The app features multiple digital and in person resources for a user's. personal journey. Sobered can be used to monitor individual progress, offer encouragement, and provide mental health resources including accountability partners, therapy, support groups, and more.

Outcomes and Lessons

This project demonstrated the role of technology to provide access to mental health resources. Between 40-60% of addicts will eventually relapse. This number can be reduced if there is a resource that is widely available to reach out for help.

I tested the prototype with 6 peers during usability testing. 6 out of 6 peers gave the app a good to very good on the five point Likert scale. After going back and correcting errors found during user testing I made my prototype open for peer review. During peer reviewing, I was made alert of design flaws and inconsistencies which were fixed with further iterations. During the iteration process, I came up with a consistent style guide for the web app where I made sure to include iOS and Gestalt design principles. 

 When it comes to the features and functionality of the app, I received a lot of positive feedback on how the app directs the user to local resources in their community. The goal behind creating the Sobered App was to make users feel supported in coping with their sobriety which can happen within or outside of the app.

Overall, the Sobered app trained my eye to be more intentional with my design choices. Through the multiple iterations that it took to create the prototype, I had to step back and make sure each design decision I was making was appealing, functional and consistent with the app as a whole. Whenever I create a product, I want to make sure that users feel seen, supported, and comfortable navigating it. I know that going forward, I will not be afraid of feedback and constructive criticism because the goal is to make sure that the design is at its best for its intended use.
